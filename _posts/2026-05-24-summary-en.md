## [80386 Microcode Disassembled: A Reverse Engineering Feat](https://www.reenigne.org/blog/80386-microcode-disassembled/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The 80386's microcode has been fully disassembled, revealing the detailed microarchitectural implementation of its complex instructions. The reverse engineering effort, documented on reenigne's blog, provides an unprecedented look at how the processor's microprogrammed control logic works. Understanding the microcode of a classic processor like the 80386 is invaluable for computer architecture enthusiasts, historians, and those studying low-level hardware design. It demystifies the trade-offs in microprogramming and instruction set implementation that influenced later CPU designs. The disassembly shows a 37-bit microcode word format split into fields such as source, destination, ALU source, ALU jump, op, sub, and bus. This work complements the z386 project, an open-source 80386 clone built using the original microcode.

hackernews · nand2mario · May 23, 12:11 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48247004)

**Background**: Microcode is a low-level layer of instructions that controls the internal sequencing of a CPU. It translates higher-level machine instructions into detailed circuit operations. The 80386 is a 32-bit processor introduced in 1985, known for its support of multitasking and protected mode. Reverse engineering microcode involves analyzing die photos or using software to extract the control store contents.

## [AI-driven HBM demand reprices consumer electronics](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/22/memory-shortage/#atom-everything) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Memory manufacturers are reallocating wafer capacity from DDR and LPDDR to HBM to meet surging AI demand, with HBM's share rising from 2% to an expected 20% by end of 2026, and each GB of HBM consuming over three times the wafer capacity of DDR or LPDDR. This shortage will significantly increase the price of consumer electronics, especially sub-$100 smartphones crucial in emerging markets, as LPDDR supply is constrained. The three remaining major memory manufacturers (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron) deliberately under-provision capacity to avoid over-investment, exacerbating the crunch for consumer memory.

rss · Simon Willison · May 22, 22:01

**Background**: HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) is a 3D-stacked memory interface used in GPUs for AI and HPC, consuming more wafer area per gigabyte than traditional DDR or LPDDR. LPDDR (Low-Power DDR) is commonly used in mobile devices for its energy efficiency. Wafer capacity is fixed in the short term, so increasing HBM allocation reduces output of other memory types.
